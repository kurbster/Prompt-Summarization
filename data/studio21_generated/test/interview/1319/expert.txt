 
-----Input----- The first line of the input contains a single integer m (1 ≤ m ≤ 200 000) — the number of primes in factorization of n.  The second line contains m primes numbers p_{i} (2 ≤ p_{i} ≤ 200 000). -----Output----- Print one integer — the product of all divisors of n modulo 10^9 + 7. -----Examples----- Input 2 2 3 Output 36 Input 3 2 3 2 Output 1728 -----Note----- In the first sample n = 2·3 = 6. The divisors of 6 are 1, 2, 3 and 6, their product is equal to 1·2·3·6 = 36. In the second sample 2·3·2 = 12. The divisors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 and 12. 1·2·3·4·6·12 = 1728.
